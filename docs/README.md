# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the central source for OctoAcme's project management processes, artifacts, and team practices. This documentation ensures all team members have equal access to how we plan, execute, and improve our projects.

---

## Purpose

- Centralize scattered project management knowledge in Copilot Spaces
- Turn tacit team insights into structured, versioned, and searchable artifacts
- Accelerate onboarding, support repeatable delivery, and reduce single-person dependencies
- Improve consistency by feeding validated improvements back into living docs

---

## Project Management Processes Overview

### Key Principles

- **Customer-first:** Always prioritize solutions that deliver measurable value
- **Iterative delivery:** Work in small, testable increments and gain early feedback
- **Clear ownership:** Project Managers and Product Managers guide planning, delivery, and quality
- **Data-informed decisions:** Outcomes are measured; we adjust and improve based on evidence
- **Psychological safety:** Questioning, feedback, and learning from mistakes are encouraged

### Lifecycle Phases

1. **Initiation:** Validate need, align stakeholders, draft a One-pager, and define measurable outcomes.
2. **Planning:** Turn approved ideas into actionable backlogs and release plans, clarify scope, and identify risks.
3. **Execution & Tracking:** Deliver through sprints, daily standups, code reviews, and regular syncs.
4. **Release & Deployment:** Ship features confidently using checklists, automation, and transparent announcements.
5. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement:** After each cycle, document what went well, what could improve, and action items.

### Roles & Communication

- **Project Manager:** Coordinates activities, risks, and communications
- **Product Manager:** Owns the vision, backlog, and outcome measurement
- **Developers:** Build, test, estimate, and identify risks
- **QA/Testing:** Validate quality and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders:** Provide inputs, approvals, and strategic context

We maintain regular routines: daily standups, weekly syncs, demos at milestones, and monthly stakeholder briefings. Escalations flow from the team to the PM, then up to the Product Lead or Sponsor as needed.

### Quality & Risk Management

- Pull request and CI standards ensure code quality
- Unit, integration, and end-to-end tests, plus manual QA as needed
- Each project tracks risks and mitigations; risks are reviewed weekly
